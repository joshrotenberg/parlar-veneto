# Chapter 19: Complex Sentences

This chapter covers subordinate clauses, relative clauses, conditionals, and other complex sentence structures in Venetian.

## 19.1 Subordinate Clauses

### Complement Clauses with *che*

Verbs of saying, thinking, knowing, and believing take complement clauses introduced by *che*:

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| *Digo **che** el vien.* | I say (that) he's coming. |
| *Penso **che** la sia brava.* | I think (that) she's good. |
| *So **che** i ga magnà.* | I know (that) they ate. |
| *Credo **che** el gabia rasón.* | I believe (that) he's right. |

Note that subjunctive forms (*sia*, *gabia*) appear after verbs of opinion and belief, as in Italian.

### Causal Clauses

*Parché* (because) introduces causal clauses:

| Venetian | Italian | English |
|----------|---------|---------|
| *No vegno **parché** stago mal.* | *Non vengo **perché** sto male.* | I'm not coming because I'm sick. |
| *El ride **parché** el xe contento.* | *Ride **perché** è contento.* | He laughs because he's happy. |

### Purpose Clauses

Purpose is expressed with *par* + infinitive or *parché* + subjunctive:

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| *Laoro **par** magnar.* | I work (in order) to eat. |
| *Te lo digo **parché** te sapia.* | I'm telling you so you know. |

### Temporal Clauses

Temporal conjunctions often take the *che* insertion discussed in Chapter 18:

| Venetian | Italian | English |
|----------|---------|---------|
| ***Quando che** el riva, magnemo.* | *Quando arriva, mangiamo.* | When he arrives, we'll eat. |
| ***Prima che** el partissa...* | *Prima che partisse...* | Before he left... |
| ***Dopo che** i ga finì...* | *Dopo che hanno finito...* | After they finished... |
| ***Fin che** no el vien...* | *Finché non viene...* | Until he comes... |
| ***Da quando che** son qua...* | *Da quando sono qui...* | Since I've been here... |

### Concessive Clauses

*Anca se* (even if) and *bènche* (although):

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| ***Anca se** piove, vago.* | Even if it rains, I'm going. |
| ***Bènche** el sia vècio, el lavora.* | Although he's old, he works. |

## 19.2 Relative Clauses

### Basic Relative Pronoun *che*

*Che* is the general-purpose relative pronoun for both subjects and direct objects:

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| *El omo **che** parla.* | The man who is speaking. |
| *El libro **che** go leto.* | The book (that) I read. |
| *La tosa **che** go visto.* | The girl (that) I saw. |

### With Prepositions

When a preposition is involved, Venetian has several options:

**Option 1: Preposition + *che* (informal)**
| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| *El posto **in che** stago.* | The place where I live. |
| *La persona **con che** parlo.* | The person I'm talking to. |

**Option 2: Using *dove che*, *cuando che*, etc.**
| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| *El posto **dove che** stago.* | The place where I live. |
| *El dì **quando che** son rivà.* | The day when I arrived. |

**Option 3: Preposition stranding (colloquial)**
| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| *El omo **che** parlo **con**.* | The man I'm talking to. |

### *Chi* for People

*Chi* can mean "whoever" or "he who":

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| ***Chi** che vol, vegna.* | Whoever wants to, come. |
| ***Chi** lavora, magna.* | He who works, eats. |
| *No so **chi** che xe.* | I don't know who it is. |

Note *chi che* with the characteristic Venetian *che* insertion.

### *Quel che* / *Queo che*

"What" (that which) is expressed with *quel che* or *queo che*:

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| *Fago **quel che** posso.* | I do what I can. |
| *Dime **queo che** te vol.* | Tell me what you want. |

## 19.3 Conditional Sentences

### Real Conditionals (Present/Future)

*Se* introduces the condition:

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| ***Se** piove, stago in casa.* | If it rains, I stay home. |
| ***Se** te vien, semo contenti.* | If you come, we'll be happy. |
| ***Se** el vol, el pol.* | If he wants to, he can. |

### Unreal Conditionals (Present)

For hypothetical present situations, use imperfect in both clauses:

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| ***Se** gavésse schei, compraría na casa.* | If I had money, I'd buy a house. |
| ***Se** el savésse, el me lo diría.* | If he knew, he'd tell me. |

### Unreal Conditionals (Past)

For contrary-to-fact past situations:

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| ***Se** gavésse savù, saría vegnù.* | If I had known, I would have come. |
| ***Se** el gavésse podù, el garía fato.* | If he could have, he would have done it. |

### Colloquial Simplification

In casual speech, the imperfect indicative often replaces both subjunctive and conditional:

| Formal | Colloquial | English |
|--------|------------|---------|
| *Se gavésse schei, compraría...* | *Se gavéa schei, comprava...* | If I had money, I'd buy... |

## 19.4 Reported Speech

### Indirect Statements

Reporting verbs (*dir*, *contar*, *pensar*) introduce indirect speech with *che*:

| Direct | Indirect |
|--------|----------|
| *"Vegno doman."* | *El ga dito **che** el vegniva doman.* |
| ("I'm coming tomorrow.") | (He said he was coming tomorrow.) |

### Tense Shifting

As in Italian, tenses shift in reported speech:

| Direct Speech | Reported Speech |
|---------------|-----------------|
| Present → Imperfect | *"Son qua"* → *che el gera là* |
| Passato prossimo → Trapassato | *"Go magnà"* → *che el gavéa magnà* |
| Future → Conditional | *"Vegnarò"* → *che el saría vegnù* |

### Indirect Questions

See Chapter 18.6 for indirect questions with *se* and question words.

## 19.5 Cleft Sentences

### *Xe... che* Construction

For emphasis, Venetian uses cleft sentences:

| Neutral | Cleft | English |
|---------|-------|---------|
| *Marco ga ciamà.* | ***Xe Marco che** ga ciamà.* | It's Marco who called. |
| *Ieri son ndà.* | ***Xe ieri che** son ndà.* | It was yesterday that I went. |

### *Quel che* for "What" Clefts

| Neutral | Cleft | English |
|---------|-------|---------|
| *Vòjo magnar.* | ***Quel che** vòjo xe magnar.* | What I want is to eat. |

## 19.6 Comparatives and Superlatives in Sentences

### Comparative Clauses with *de quel che*

"Than" in comparative clauses:

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| *El xe pi alto **de quel che** pensava.* | He's taller than I thought. |
| *Xe pi difissile **de quel che** par.* | It's harder than it seems. |

### Equative with *come che*

Note the *che* insertion:

| Venetian | Italian | English |
|----------|---------|---------|
| *Fago **come che** me par.* | *Faccio **come** mi pare.* | I do as I please. |
| *Xe **come che** te go dito.* | *È **come** ti ho detto.* | It's like I told you. |

## 19.7 Coordination of Clauses

### *E*, *Ma*, *O*

Basic coordination:

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| *El magna **e** el béve.* | He eats and drinks. |
| *Vòjo vegnir, **ma** no posso.* | I want to come, but I can't. |
| *Te vien **o** te stà?* | Are you coming or staying? |

### *Né... né*

"Neither... nor":

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| ***Né** el parla **né** el scolte.* | He neither speaks nor listens. |
| *No go **né** schei **né** tempo.* | I have neither money nor time. |

### *O... o*

"Either... or":

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| ***O** te vien **o** te stà.* | Either you come or you stay. |
| ***O** desso **o** mai.* | Either now or never. |

## 19.8 Word Order in Complex Sentences

### Clitic Placement

In subordinate clauses, clitics precede the verb as in main clauses:

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| *So che **te** magni.* | I know (that) you eat. |
| *Quando **el** vien...* | When he comes... |
| *Parché **i** parla?* | Why are they talking? |

### With Infinitives

Clitics can attach to infinitives:

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| *Vòjo **véder-lo**.* | I want to see him. |
| *Par **dir-te** la verità...* | To tell you the truth... |

Or remain before the modal:

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| ***Lo** vòjo véder.* | I want to see him. |
| ***Te** vòjo dir...* | I want to tell you... |

## Summary

### Key Conjunctions

| Venetian | Italian | English |
|----------|---------|---------|
| *che* | *che* | that |
| *parché* | *perché* | because/why |
| *se* | *se* | if |
| *quando (che)* | *quando* | when |
| *dove (che)* | *dove* | where |
| *come (che)* | *come* | how/as |
| *fin che* | *finché* | until |
| *anca se* | *anche se* | even if |
| *bènche* | *benché* | although |

### The *Che* Insertion Pattern

Venetian characteristically inserts *che* after:
- Temporal conjunctions: *quando che*, *fin che*, *da quando che*
- Locative expressions: *dove che*
- Manner expressions: *come che*
- Relative *chi*: *chi che*

This feature is absent in standard Italian and serves as a reliable marker of Venetian syntax.

### Diagnostic Sentence

> *No so quando che el riva in bar.*
> (I don't know when he's arriving at the bar.)

This single sentence contains three Venetian markers:
1. *quando che* (not *quando*)
2. *in bar* (not *al bar*)  
3. Clitic *el* before verb

Compare Italian: *Non so quando arriva al bar.*
