# Chapter 16: Non-Finite Forms

Non-finite forms - the infinitive, gerund, and participles - don't show person or number. They work as verbal nouns, adjectives, or in auxiliary constructions. Venetian non-finite forms closely parallel Italian but with characteristic phonological differences.

## 16.1 The Infinitive

The infinitive is the "dictionary form" of the verb, used in many constructions.

### Form

Venetian infinitives end in *-ar*, *-er*, or *-ir*:

| Venetian | Italian | English |
|----------|---------|---------|
| *magnar* | *mangiare* | to eat |
| *parlar* | *parlare* | to speak |
| *véder* | *vedere* | to see |
| *scriver* | *scrivere* | to write |
| *partir* | *partire* | to leave |
| *dormir* | *dormire* | to sleep |

Note: Italian *-are* becomes *-ar*, reflecting the characteristic loss of final vowels.

### Uses of the Infinitive

**After modal verbs (*poder*, *voler*, *dover*, *saver*):**
- *Posso magnar?* (Can I eat?)
- *Vòjo partir.* (I want to leave.)
- *Devo andar.* (I have to go.)
- *No so far queło.* (I don't know how to do that.)

**After verbs of perception:**
- *Go visto rivar el treno.* (I saw the train arrive.)
- *Sento cantar i oxełi.* (I hear the birds singing.)

**After *far* and *lassar*:**
- *Fame saver.* (Let me know.)
- *Làsseme parlar!* (Let me speak!)
- *El me ga fato spetar.* (He made me wait.)

**After prepositions:**
- *Son qua par magnar.* (I'm here to eat.)
- *Prima de partir...* (Before leaving...)
- *Sensa dir gnente...* (Without saying anything...)
- *Dopo aver magnà...* (After having eaten...)

**As subject or object:**
- *Magnar xe necessario.* (Eating is necessary.)
- *Bevér massa no xe bon.* (Drinking too much isn't good.)
- *Me piase laorar.* (I like to work.)

**With *da* (expressing necessity or purpose):**
- *Ghe xe tanto da far.* (There's so much to do.)
- *Xe na roba da véder.* (It's something to see.)
- *Acqua da bever.* (Drinking water.)

**In negative commands (2sg):**
- *No parlar!* (Don't speak!)
- *No tocar!* (Don't touch!)

### Compound Infinitive

The past infinitive is formed with *aver/gavér* or *èsser* + past participle:

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| *aver magnà* | to have eaten |
| *èsser ndà* | to have gone |
| *gavér dito* | to have said |
| *èsser rivà* | to have arrived |

Used after prepositions for completed actions:
- *Dopo aver magnà, son ndà fora.* (After having eaten, I went out.)
- *Sensa aver capìo gnente...* (Without having understood anything...)

## 16.2 The Gerund

The gerund expresses ongoing or simultaneous action.

### Formation

Add *-ando* to -ar stems, *-endo* to -er/-ir stems:

| Infinitive | Gerund | English |
|------------|--------|---------|
| *magnar* | *magnando* | eating |
| *parlar* | *parlando* | speaking |
| *véder* | *vedendo* | seeing |
| *scriver* | *scrivendo* | writing |
| *partir* | *partendo* | leaving |
| *dormir* | *dormendo* | sleeping |

### Irregular Gerunds

| Infinitive | Gerund |
|------------|--------|
| *èsser* | *essendo* |
| *gavér* | *gavendo* / *avendo* |
| *far* | *fasendo* / *fazendo* |
| *dir* | *disendo* / *dixendo* |

### Uses of the Gerund

**Simultaneous action:**
- *El magna vardando la TV.* (He eats while watching TV.)
- *Son rivà corendo.* (I arrived running.)

**Cause or reason:**
- *Essendo stanco, me son sentà.* (Being tired, I sat down.)
- *No gavendo schei, no posso comprar gnente.* (Not having money, I can't buy anything.)

**Manner:**
- *El ga risposto ridendo.* (He answered laughing.)
- *La xe entrà pianxendo.* (She came in crying.)

**Progressive construction (*star* + gerund):**
- *Cossa te stè fasendo?* (What are you doing?)
- *Stago magnando.* (I'm eating.)
- *El stava dormendo.* (He was sleeping.)
- *Stavimo parlando de ti.* (We were talking about you.)

The progressive with *star* is very common in Venetian, even more than in Italian.

### Compound Gerund

The past gerund uses *avendo/gavendo* or *essendo* + past participle:

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| *avendo magnà* | having eaten |
| *essendo partìo* | having left |
| *gavendo finìo* | having finished |

- *Avendo magnà, son ndà via.* (Having eaten, I left.)
- *Essendo rivài tardi, no i ga trovà posto.* (Having arrived late, they didn't find a seat.)

## 16.3 The Past Participle

The past participle is used in compound tenses, as an adjective, and in passive constructions.

### Regular Formation

| Infinitive Class | Ending | Example |
|------------------|--------|---------|
| -ar | *-à* | *magnà* (eaten), *parlà* (spoken) |
| -er | *-ù* | *vendù* (sold), *savù* (known) |
| -ir | *-ì* / *-ìo* | *dormì* (slept), *partìo* (left) |

### Irregular Past Participles

Many common verbs have irregular participles (see also Chapter 12):

| Infinitive | Past Participle | English |
|------------|-----------------|---------|
| *èsser* | *stà* / *stài* | been |
| *far* | *fato* | done, made |
| *dir* | *dito* | said |
| *véder* | *visto* | seen |
| *scriver* | *scrito* | written |
| *léxer* | *leto* | read |
| *tòr* | *tolto* | taken |
| *meter* | *messo* / *metù* | put |
| *romper* | *roto* | broken |
| *vènder* | *vendù* | sold |
| *pèrder* | *perso* / *perdù* | lost |
| *risponder* | *risposto* | answered |
| *nàsser* | *nassù* | born |
| *morir* | *morto* | died |
| *còrer* | *corso* | run |
| *pianxer* | *pianto* | cried |
| *vìnser* | *vinto* | won |
| *vegnir* | *vegnù* | come |
| *andar* | *ndà* / *andà* | gone |

### Agreement

**With *èsser* auxiliary:** The participle agrees in gender and number:

| Subject | Form | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| m. sg. | *-à/-ù/-ì* | *El xe ndà.* |
| f. sg. | *-àa/-ùa/-ìa* | *La xe ndàa.* |
| m. pl. | *-ài/-ùi/-ìi* | *I xe ndài.* |
| f. pl. | *-àe/-ùe/-ìe* | *Le xe ndàe.* |

**With *gavér* auxiliary:** Usually no agreement, but agrees with preceding direct object:

- *Go magnà la torta.* (I ate the cake.) - no agreement
- *La torta? La go magnàa.* (The cake? I ate it.) - agreement

### Uses of the Past Participle

**In compound tenses:**
- *Go magnà.* (I have eaten.)
- *Son partìo.* (I have left.)
- *Gavéa visto.* (I had seen.)

**As adjective:**
- *la porta verta* (the open door)
- *el libro scrito in veneto* (the book written in Venetian)
- *i puti stanchi* (the tired children)
- *le finestre rote* (the broken windows)

**In absolute constructions:**
- *Finìo el laoro, son ndà a casa.* (Work finished, I went home.)
- *Dito queo, el xe partìo.* (Having said that, he left.)

**In passive constructions (see below):**
- *El libro xe stà scrito in veneto.* (The book was written in Venetian.)

## 16.4 The Present Participle

The present participle is less common than in Italian. It functions mainly as an adjective.

### Formation

Add *-ante* (for -ar verbs) or *-ente* (for -er/-ir verbs) to the stem:

| Infinitive | Present Participle | English |
|------------|-------------------|---------|
| *parlar* | *parlante* | speaking |
| *interessar* | *interessante* | interesting |
| *véder* | *vedente* | seeing |
| *seguir* | *seguente* | following |
| *sofrir* | *sofrente* | suffering |

### Uses

Mainly as adjectives:
- *acqua corente* (running water)
- *la setimana seguente* (the following week)
- *un film interessante* (an interesting film)

The gerund is preferred for verbal uses:
- *Vedendo queło...* (Seeing that...) - not *vedente*

## 16.5 Passive Constructions

The passive voice uses *èsser* + past participle:

### Formation

| Tense | Active | Passive |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Present | *El magna la torta.* | *La torta xe magnàa.* |
| Passato prossimo | *El ga magnà la torta.* | *La torta xe stàa magnàa.* |
| Imperfect | *El magnava la torta.* | *La torta gera magnàa.* |
| Future | *El magnarà la torta.* | *La torta sarà magnàa.* |

### With Agent (*da*)

The agent is introduced by *da*:
- *El libro xe stà scrito da un veneto.* (The book was written by a Venetian.)
- *La casa xe stàa costruìa da me nono.* (The house was built by my grandfather.)

### *Vegnir* as Passive Auxiliary

*Vegnir* can replace *èsser* in the passive, especially for habitual or general statements:

| With *èsser* | With *vegnir* |
|--------------|---------------|
| *Xe magnà.* | *Vien magnà.* |
| *Xe parlà.* | *Vien parlà.* |

- *El veneto el vien parlà in tuto el Veneto.* (Venetian is spoken throughout the Veneto.)
- *Sti dolsi i vien fati par Nadae.* (These sweets are made for Christmas.)

### Impersonal *se*

An alternative to the passive is the impersonal *se*:
- *Qua se parla veneto.* (Venetian is spoken here.)
- *Come se dise?* (How do you say it? / How is it said?)
- *No se sa mai.* (You never know. / It's never known.)

## Summary

### Infinitive

| Type | Example | Use |
|------|---------|-----|
| Simple | *magnar*, *véder*, *partir* | After modals, prepositions, as noun |
| Compound | *aver magnà*, *èsser ndà* | Past infinitive after *dopo*, *sensa* |

### Gerund

| Type | Formation | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Present | *-ando*, *-endo* | *magnando*, *vedendo* |
| Compound | aux. gerund + participle | *avendo magnà*, *essendo ndà* |

Progressive: *star* + gerund (*stago magnando*)

### Participles

| Type | Formation | Use |
|------|-----------|-----|
| Past | *-à*, *-ù*, *-ì/-ìo* | Compound tenses, adjectives, passive |
| Present | *-ante*, *-ente* | Adjectives (rare as verb) |

### Passive

| Construction | Example |
|--------------|---------|
| *èsser* + participle | *xe stà scrito* |
| *vegnir* + participle | *vien parlà* |
| Impersonal *se* | *se parla veneto* |
