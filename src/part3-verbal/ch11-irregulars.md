# Chapter 11: Common Irregular Verbs

Every language has verbs that refuse to follow the rules. In Venetian, as in Italian, the most frequently used verbs are often irregular. This chapter covers the essential irregular verbs you will encounter constantly in everyday speech.

The auxiliaries *èsser* (to be) and *gavér/aver* (to have) were covered in Chapter 10. Here we treat the remaining high-frequency irregulars.

## 11.1 Andar (to go)

One of the most common verbs in any language. Venetian *andar* is irregular in the present and has a suppletive past participle.

### Present Indicative

| Person | Form | English |
|--------|------|---------|
| mi | *vago* | I go |
| ti | *te va* | you go |
| lu/ela | *el/la va* | he/she goes |
| nu | *ndemo* / *andemo* | we go |
| vu | *ndè* / *andè* | you (pl.) go |
| lori/lore | *i/le va* | they go |

**Notes:**
- 1sg *vago* (not *ando*)
- 2sg and 3sg/pl use the short stem *va*
- 1pl and 2pl have two forms: *ndemo/ndè* (more common colloquially) and *andemo/andè*

### Imperative

| Person | Form | Example |
|--------|------|---------|
| ti | *va!* | *Va a casa!* (Go home!) |
| nu | *ndemo!* | *Ndemo!* (Let's go!) |
| vu | *ndè!* | *Ndè via!* (Go away!) |

The exclamation *Ndemo!* is extremely common - you will hear it constantly.

### Past Participle

*ndà* / *andà* - gone

- *Son ndà a Padova.* (I went to Padua.)
- *El xe andà via.* (He left. / He went away.)

### Common Expressions

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| *Vago a casa.* | I'm going home. |
| *Dove te va?* | Where are you going? |
| *Ndemo!* | Let's go! |
| *Va ben.* | It's fine. / Okay. |
| *Cossa va?* | What's up? / How's it going? |
| *No la va.* | It's not working. / Things aren't going well. |
| *Va a cagar!* | Go to hell! (vulgar) |

## 11.2 Far (to do, to make)

Another essential verb, used even more broadly than Italian *fare*.

### Present Indicative

| Person | Form | English |
|--------|------|---------|
| mi | *fago* / *fasso* | I do/make |
| ti | *te fa* | you do/make |
| lu/ela | *el/la fa* | he/she does/makes |
| nu | *femo* | we do/make |
| vu | *fè* | you (pl.) do/make |
| lori/lore | *i/le fa* | they do/make |

**Notes:**
- 1sg has two forms: *fago* (more Paduan) and *fasso* (more Venetian)
- The stem *fa* appears in 2sg, 3sg, and 3pl

### Imperative

| Person | Form | Example |
|--------|------|---------|
| ti | *fa!* | *Fa presto!* (Hurry up!) |
| nu | *femo!* | *Femo festa!* (Let's party!) |
| vu | *fè!* | *Fè atension!* (Pay attention!) |

### Past Participle

*fato* - done, made

- *Cossa te ghe fato?* (What did you do to him?)
- *Go fato ben.* (I did well.)

### Common Expressions

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| *Cossa feto?* | What are you doing? |
| *Fa caldo.* | It's hot. (weather) |
| *Fa fredo.* | It's cold. |
| *Femo cussì.* | Let's do it this way. |
| *Fa gnente.* | It doesn't matter. / Never mind. |
| *Farse in qua* | to make room |
| *Far finta* | to pretend |
| *Far tabaco* | to make a ruckus (lit. "make tobacco") |

## 11.3 Dir (to say, to tell)

Essential for reported speech and everyday communication.

### Present Indicative

| Person | Form | English |
|--------|------|---------|
| mi | *digo* | I say |
| ti | *te disi* | you say |
| lu/ela | *el/la dise* | he/she says |
| nu | *disemo* | we say |
| vu | *disì* | you (pl.) say |
| lori/lore | *i/le dise* | they say |

**Notes:**
- The stem alternates between *dig-* (1sg) and *dis-* (elsewhere)
- Follows a pattern similar to Italian *dire*

### Imperative

| Person | Form | Example |
|--------|------|---------|
| ti | *dì!* | *Dì la verità!* (Tell the truth!) |
| nu | *disemo!* | *Disemoghe!* (Let's tell him!) |
| vu | *disì!* | *Disì!* (Tell!) |

### Past Participle

*dito* - said, told

- *Te go dito de no.* (I told you no.)
- *Cossa te ghe dito?* (What did you say to him?)

### Common Expressions

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| *Cossa vol dir?* | What does it mean? |
| *Come se dise...?* | How do you say...? |
| *A dir la verità...* | To tell the truth... |
| *Digo mi!* | I'll say! / You're telling me! |
| *No star dirme!* | Don't tell me! |
| *Cussì i dise.* | That's what they say. |

## 11.4 Star (to stay, to be)

While *èsser* is the main "to be" verb, *star* is used for states, conditions, and location. It also forms the progressive with *drio*.

### Present Indicative

| Person | Form | English |
|--------|------|---------|
| mi | *stago* | I stay/am |
| ti | *te sta* | you stay/are |
| lu/ela | *el/la sta* | he/she stays/is |
| nu | *stemo* | we stay/are |
| vu | *stè* | you (pl.) stay/are |
| lori/lore | *i/le sta* | they stay/are |

### Imperative

| Person | Form | Example |
|--------|------|---------|
| ti | *sta!* | *Sta fermo!* (Stay still!) |
| nu | *stemo!* | *Stemo qua!* (Let's stay here!) |
| vu | *stè!* | *Stè calmi!* (Stay calm!) |

### Past Participle

*stà* - stayed, been

- *Son stà a casa.* (I stayed home.)
- *Come te si stà?* (How have you been?)

### Star vs. Èsser

| Use *star* for: | Use *èsser* for: |
|-----------------|------------------|
| Health: *Come steto?* | Identity: *Son veneto.* |
| Location (temporary): *Stago qua.* | Location (permanent): *Venessia la xe in Italia.* |
| States: *Sta sito!* (Be quiet!) | Attributes: *El xe alto.* |
| Progressive: *Stago drio magnar.* | Definition: *Cos'èło?* |

### Common Expressions

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| *Come steto?* | How are you? |
| *Stago ben.* | I'm fine. |
| *Sta atento!* | Pay attention! / Be careful! |
| *Sta sito!* | Be quiet! |
| *Star drio* | to be in the middle of (doing) |
| *Star fora* | to stay outside |
| *No star...* | Don't... (negative imperative) |

## 11.5 Véder (to see)

Shows the typical second-conjugation vowel alternation.

### Present Indicative

| Person | Form | English |
|--------|------|---------|
| mi | *védo* | I see |
| ti | *te vidi* | you see |
| lu/ela | *el/la véde* | he/she sees |
| nu | *vedemo* | we see |
| vu | *vedì* | you (pl.) see |
| lori/lore | *i/le véde* | they see |

**Note:** The stressed *é* becomes *i* in unstressed positions (2sg).

### Past Participle

*visto* / *vedù* - seen

- *Te go visto ieri.* (I saw you yesterday.)
- *No go mai vedù gnente.* (I've never seen anything.)

### Common Expressions

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| *Vuto véder?* | Do you want to see? |
| *Se véde che...* | It's obvious that... / You can tell that... |
| *Vedemo!* | Let's see! / We'll see! |
| *No me védo.* | I can't see myself. |
| *Farse véder* | to show up, to make an appearance |

## 11.6 Saér / Savér (to know)

Irregular throughout. Compare Italian *sapere*.

### Present Indicative

| Person | Form | English |
|--------|------|---------|
| mi | *so* | I know |
| ti | *te sa* | you know |
| lu/ela | *el/la sa* | he/she knows |
| nu | *savemo* | we know |
| vu | *savì* | you (pl.) know |
| lori/lore | *i/le sa* | they know |

**Note:** The singular forms are monosyllabic (*so*, *sa*), while plurals use the full stem *sav-*.

### Past Participle

*savù* / *sapù* - known

- *L'go savù doman.* (I found out tomorrow.)

### Common Expressions

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| *No so.* | I don't know. |
| *Chi sa?* | Who knows? |
| *Seto cossa...?* | Do you know what...? |
| *Savér far* | to know how to do |
| *No so cossa dir.* | I don't know what to say. |
| *Savérsela* | to manage, to get by |

## 11.7 Poder (to be able, can)

Modal verb, essential for expressing ability and possibility.

### Present Indicative

| Person | Form | English |
|--------|------|---------|
| mi | *posso* | I can |
| ti | *te pòi* | you can |
| lu/ela | *el/la pòl* | he/she can |
| nu | *podemo* | we can |
| vu | *podì* | you (pl.) can |
| lori/lore | *i/le pòl* | they can |

**Notes:**
- 2sg and 3sg/pl have unique forms (*pòi*, *pòl*)
- Some speakers use *pol* without the accent
- 1sg *posso* parallels Italian

### Past Participle

*podù* / *possù* - been able

- *No go podù vegnir.* (I couldn't come.)

### Usage

*Poder* + infinitive:

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| *Posso vegnir?* | Can I come? |
| *No te pòi far cussì.* | You can't do that. |
| *Podemo ndar?* | Can we go? |
| *Se pol?* | Is it allowed? / May one? |

## 11.8 Voler (to want)

Another essential modal verb.

### Present Indicative

| Person | Form | English |
|--------|------|---------|
| mi | *vòjo* | I want |
| ti | *te vòi* | you want |
| lu/ela | *el/la vòl* | he/she wants |
| nu | *volemo* | we want |
| vu | *volì* | you (pl.) want |
| lori/lore | *i/le vòl* | they want |

**Notes:**
- Some areas use *vògio* for 1sg
- 2sg and 3sg/pl share the stem *vò-*

### Contracted Question Forms

| Full | Contracted | English |
|------|------------|---------|
| *Te vòi?* | *Vuto?* | Do you want? |
| *El vòl?* | *Vòlo?* | Does he want? |

The contracted *Vuto?* is extremely common:
- *Vuto magnar?* (Do you want to eat?)
- *Vuto véder?* (Do you want to see?)
- *Vuto vegnir?* (Do you want to come?)

### Past Participle

*volù* / *vossù* - wanted

- *Go volù farlo.* (I wanted to do it.)

### Common Expressions

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| *Cossa vuto?* | What do you want? |
| *Voler ben* | to love, to care for |
| *Voler dir* | to mean |
| *Cossa vol dir?* | What does it mean? |
| *Come te vòi.* | As you wish. |

## 11.9 Vegnir (to come)

Irregular, with a stem change in the present.

### Present Indicative

| Person | Form | English |
|--------|------|---------|
| mi | *végno* | I come |
| ti | *te vien* | you come |
| lu/ela | *el/la vien* | he/she comes |
| nu | *vegnimo* | we come |
| vu | *vegnì* | you (pl.) come |
| lori/lore | *i/le vien* | they come |

**Note:** The stem alternates between *végn-* and *vien-*.

### Imperative

| Person | Form | Example |
|--------|------|---------|

| nu | *vegnimo!* | *Vegnimo!* (Let's come!) |

### Past Participle

*vegnù* - come

- *Son vegnù a véderlo.* (I came to see him.)

### Common Expressions

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| *Te vien?* | Are you coming? |

| *Vegnir fora* | to come out |
| *Vegnir drio* | to follow |
| *Vegnir su* | to come up, to grow up |
| *Me vien da rider.* | I feel like laughing. |

## Summary: Irregular Verb Quick Reference

### Present Tense Stems

| Verb | 1sg | 2/3sg | 1pl | Meaning |
|------|-----|-------|-----|---------|
| andar | vago | va | ndemo | to go |
| far | fago/fasso | fa | femo | to do/make |
| dir | digo | dise | disemo | to say |
| star | stago | sta | stemo | to stay |
| véder | védo | véde | vedemo | to see |
| saér | so | sa | savemo | to know |
| poder | posso | pòl | podemo | can |
| voler | vòjo | vòl | volemo | to want |
| vegnir | végno | vien | vegnimo | to come |
| tòr | tògo | tòl | togemo | to take |
| meter | méto | méte | metemo | to put |
| dar | dago | da | demo | to give |

### Past Participles

| Verb | Past Participle |
|------|-----------------|
| andar | ndà / andà |
| far | fato |
| dir | dito |
| star | stà |
| véder | visto / vedù |
| saér | savù / sapù |
| poder | podù / possù |
| voler | volù / vossù |
| vegnir | vegnù |
| tòr | tolto |
| meter | méso / metù |
| dar | dà / dato |

### Key Points

1. **Stem alternation** is common: different stems for 1sg vs. 2/3 person
2. **Monosyllabic forms** appear in 2sg and 3sg/pl (*va*, *fa*, *sa*, *sta*, *da*)
3. **Modal verbs** (*poder*, *voler*) take an infinitive: *Posso ndar?*
4. **The clitic system** still applies: *el va*, *te sa*, *i vol*
5. **Contracted forms** in questions: *Vuto?* (Do you want?)
6. **Regional variation** exists, especially in 1sg forms
