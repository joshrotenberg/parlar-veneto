# Chapter 9: Present Indicative

The present tense is the foundation of the verb system - the tense you need for everyday communication. This chapter provides complete conjugation paradigms for regular verbs in all three classes, with careful attention to the interaction between verb endings and subject clitics.

## 9.1 First Conjugation (-ar)

First conjugation verbs are the most common. The model verb is *parlar* (to speak).

### Complete Paradigm: *parlar* (to speak)

| Person | Full Form | Short Form | English |
|--------|-----------|------------|---------|
| mi | *parlo* | *parlo* | I speak |
| ti | *ti te parli* | *te parli* | you speak |
| lu | *lu el parla* | *el parla* | he speaks |
| ela | *ela la parla* | *la parla* | she speaks |
| nu | *nu parlemo* | *parlemo* | we speak |
| vu | *vu parlè* | *parlè* | you (pl.) speak |
| lori | *lori i parla* | *i parla* | they (m.) speak |
| lore | *lore le parla* | *le parla* | they (f.) speak |

**Notes:**

- The emphatic pronoun (*mi*, *ti*, *lu*, etc.) is optional
- The clitic (*te*, *el*, *la*, *i*, *le*) is **required** for 2nd and 3rd person
- 3sg and 3pl have identical verb forms (*parla*) - the clitic distinguishes them
- 1sg and 1pl have no clitic

### Endings Summary: First Conjugation

|--------|--------|
| 1sg | -o |
| 2sg | -i |
| 3sg | -a |
| 1pl | -emo |
| 2pl | -è |
| 3pl | -a |

## 9.4 Regional Variation

Verb endings vary across the Veneto. The forms above represent central Venetian (Padovano). Here are some variations:

### Second Person Singular

| Region | Clitic | *parlar* |
|--------|--------|----------|
| Venice | *ti* | *ti parli* |
| Padua-Vicenza | *te* | *te parli* |
| Treviso-Belluno | *te/tu* | *te parla* / *te magna* |

Note that in Treviso-Belluno, the 2sg verb form often matches 3sg (*parla* instead of *parli*).

### Third Person Singular

| Region | Clitic | *sentir* |
|--------|--------|----------|
| Most areas | *el/la* | *el sente* |
| Belluno | *al/la* | *al sent* |

### The Clitic is Key

Despite these variations, the clitic system is universal. *Te* (or *ti* in Venice) always marks 2sg; *el/la* always marks 3sg. The specific verb ending matters less than the clitic.

## 9.5 Usage Patterns

### Simple Present

As in Italian, the present tense expresses:

**Current actions:**

- *Cossa te feto?* (What are you doing?)
- *Magno un tramezzin.* (I'm eating a sandwich.)

**General truths:**

- *L'aqua la boje a sento gradi.* (Water boils at 100 degrees.)
- *El sol el leva a est.* (The sun rises in the east.)

**Habitual actions:**

- *Laoro tute le matine.* (I work every morning.)
- *El va a Venessia ogni setimana.* (He goes to Venice every week.)

### Progressive with *èsser drio*

For emphasis on ongoing action, Venetian uses *èsser drio* (to be behind/in the middle of):

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| *Son drio magnar.* | I'm (in the middle of) eating. |
| *Te si drio laorar?* | Are you working (right now)? |
| *El xe drio parlar al telefono.* | He's talking on the phone. |

This construction emphasizes that the action is happening at this very moment.

### Near Future

Like Italian, the present can express near future:

- *Doman parto par Padova.* (Tomorrow I leave for Padua.)
- *Stasera magnemo fora.* (Tonight we're eating out.)

## 9.6 Questions in the Present

### Yes/No Questions: Inversion

For yes/no questions, the clitic moves after the verb and changes form:

| Statement | Question |
|-----------|----------|
| *Te parli.* | *Parli-to?* (Do you speak?) |
| *El magna.* | *Magna-lo?* (Does he eat?) |
| *La parte.* | *Parte-la?* (Is she leaving?) |
| *I lavora.* | *Lavora-li?* (Do they work?) |

The inverted clitics: *te* → *-to*, *el* → *-lo*, *la* → *-la*, *i* → *-li*, *le* → *-le*

### Information Questions

With question words, the clitic stays in normal position:

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| *Cossa te magni?* | *Magno pasta.* |
| *Dove el va?* | *El va a casa.* |
| *Quando i riva?* | *I riva doman.* |
| *Parché te parli cussì?* | *Parché son veneto!* |

## 9.7 Negation

Negation uses *no* before the clitic:

| Affirmative | Negative |
|-------------|----------|
| *Parlo.* | *No parlo.* |
| *Te parli.* | *No te parli.* |
| *El parla.* | *No el parla.* |
| *Parlemo.* | *No parlemo.* |
| *I parla.* | *No i parla.* |

In questions with inversion:

| Question | Negative Question |
|----------|-------------------|
| *Parli-to?* | *No parli-to?* |
| *Magna-lo?* | *No magna-lo?* |

## Summary

### Present Tense Endings

| Person | -ar | -er | -ir (A) | -ir (B) |
|--------|-----|-----|---------|---------|
| 1sg | -o | -o | -o | -iso |
| 2sg | -i | -i | -i | -isi |
| 3sg | -a | -e | -e | -ise |
| 1pl | -emo | -emo | -imo | -imo |
| 2pl | -è | -ì | -ì | -ì |
| 3pl | -a | -e | -e | -ise |

### Key Points

1. **Clitics are obligatory** for 2nd and 3rd person
2. **Vowel alternation** in 2nd conjugation: stressed é/ó → unstressed i/u
3. **Two types** of -ir verbs: simple and inchoative (-is-)
4. **Question inversion** moves clitic after verb: *te* → *-to*, *el* → *-lo*
5. **Negation** with *no* before the clitic
6. **Progressive** with *èsser drio* + infinitive
