# Chapter 14: Subjunctive

The subjunctive (*congiuntivo*) expresses doubt, possibility, desire, emotion, and other non-factual meanings. While it exists in Venetian, it's used less frequently than in standard Italian, often replaced by the indicative or infinitive constructions in casual speech. Still, understanding the subjunctive is essential for formal contexts and for recognizing it in proverbs and set expressions.

## 14.1 Present Subjunctive

### Formation

The present subjunctive is formed from the first person singular present indicative stem with distinctive endings.

**-ar verbs (*magnar*):**

| Person | Form | English |
|--------|------|---------|
| mi | *che magna* | that I eat |
| ti | *che te magni* | that you eat |
| lu/ela | *che 'l magna* | that he/she eat |
| nu | *che magnemo* | that we eat |
| vu | *che magné* | that you (pl.) eat |
| lori/lore | *che i magna* | that they eat |

**-er verbs (*véder*):**

| Person | Form | English |
|--------|------|---------|
| mi | *che veda* | that I see |
| ti | *che te vedi* | that you see |
| lu/ela | *che 'l veda* | that he/she see |
| nu | *che vedemo* | that we see |
| vu | *che vedé* | that you (pl.) see |
| lori/lore | *che i veda* | that they see |

**-ir verbs (*partir*):**

| Person | Form | English |
|--------|------|---------|
| mi | *che parta* | that I leave |
| ti | *che te parti* | that you leave |
| lu/ela | *che 'l parta* | that he/she leave |
| nu | *che partimo* | that we leave |
| vu | *che partì* | that you (pl.) leave |
| lori/lore | *che i parta* | that they leave |

## 14.3 Uses of the Subjunctive

### After Verbs of Desire, Will, Command

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| *Vòjo che 'l vegna.* | I want him to come. |
| *Spero che la staga ben.* | I hope she's well. |
| *Preferisso che i resta qua.* | I prefer that they stay here. |
| *Ordino che 'l parta.* | I order him to leave. |

### After Verbs of Doubt, Belief, Opinion

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| *Credo che 'l sia malà.* | I think he's sick. |
| *No penso che i gabia rason.* | I don't think they're right. |
| *Dubito che 'l vegna.* | I doubt he'll come. |
| *Par che piova.* | It looks like it's raining. |

### After Impersonal Expressions

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| *Bisogna che 'l vaga.* | He needs to go. |
| *Xe meio che te staga sito.* | It's better that you stay quiet. |
| *Xe possibiłe che i riva tardi.* | It's possible they'll arrive late. |
| *Xe un pecà che no 'l sia qua.* | It's a pity he's not here. |

### After Certain Conjunctions

| Conjunction | Example |
|-------------|---------|
| *parché* (so that) | *Te lo digo parché te sapia.* (I'm telling you so you know.) |
| *prima che* | *Prima che 'l parta...* (Before he leaves...) |
| *sensa che* | *Sensa che nissun sapia.* (Without anyone knowing.) |
| *bènche* | *Bènche 'l sia vècio...* (Although he's old...) |
| *a meno che* | *A meno che no 'l vegna...* (Unless he comes...) |

### In Conditional Sentences

The imperfect subjunctive appears in the "if" clause of unreal conditions:

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| *Se gavésse schei, comprarìa na casa.* | If I had money, I'd buy a house. |
| *Se fusse ti, andarìa.* | If I were you, I'd go. |
| *Se podésse, vegnarìa.* | If I could, I'd come. |
| *Se 'l savésse, el me lo dirìa.* | If he knew, he'd tell me. |

### In Relative Clauses (with indefinite antecedent)

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| *Sérco un che sapia parlar veneto.* | I'm looking for someone who knows Venetian. |
| *No ghe xe nissun che possa far queło.* | There's no one who can do that. |

## 14.4 Subjunctive vs. Indicative

In casual spoken Venetian, the indicative often replaces the subjunctive:

| Formal (Subjunctive) | Colloquial (Indicative) |
|----------------------|-------------------------|
| *Credo che 'l sia malà.* | *Credo che 'l xe malà.* |
| *Spero che 'l vegna.* | *Spero che 'l vien.* |
| *Penso che i gabia rason.* | *Penso che i ga rason.* |

This shift is more pronounced in Venetian than in standard Italian. However, the subjunctive remains in:
- Set expressions and proverbs
- Formal or careful speech
- After *bisogna che*, *parché* (purpose), *prima che*
- In conditional sentences

### Infinitive Instead of Subjunctive

When the subject is the same, the infinitive is preferred:

| With Subjunctive (diff. subjects) | With Infinitive (same subject) |
|-----------------------------------|--------------------------------|
| *Vòjo che 'l vegna.* (I want him to come.) | *Vòjo vegnir.* (I want to come.) |
| *Spero che 'l finissa.* (I hope he finishes.) | *Spero de finir.* (I hope to finish.) |

## 14.5 Pluperfect Subjunctive

The pluperfect subjunctive expresses hypothetical past situations. It's formed with the imperfect subjunctive of the auxiliary plus the past participle.

### Formation

| With *gavér* | With *èsser* |
|--------------|--------------|
| *gavésse magnà* | *fusse ndà* |
| *te gavéssi parlà* | *te fussi rivà* |
| *el gavésse visto* | *el fusse partìo* |
| *gavéssimo finìo* | *fùssimo vegnùi* |
| *gavéssi dito* | *fussi restài* |
| *i gavésse fato* | *i fusse tornài* |

### Use in Past Contrary-to-Fact Conditions

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| *Se gavésse savù, sarìa vegnù.* | If I had known, I would have come. |
| *Se fusse stà là, gavarìa visto tuto.* | If I had been there, I would have seen everything. |
| *Se no 'l gavésse piovù, sarìsimo ndài.* | If it hadn't rained, we would have gone. |

## 14.6 Subjunctive in Proverbs and Expressions

The subjunctive survives in traditional sayings:

| Proverb | Translation |
|---------|-------------|
| *Che Dio ne scampa!* | God save us! |
| *Che sia quel che sia.* | Whatever will be, will be. |
| *Viva chi che sia!* | Long live whoever! |
| *Magari fusse vero!* | If only it were true! |
| *Volesse el sielo!* | Heaven willing! |
| *Sia come che sia.* | Be that as it may. |

### Common Expressions

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| *Che vegna!* | Let him come! |
| *Che i faga quel che i vol!* | Let them do what they want! |
| *Che Dio vogia!* | God willing! |
| *Cossa vòtu che te diga?* | What do you want me to say? |

## Summary

### Present Subjunctive Patterns

| Verb Type | 1sg | 3sg | 3pl |
|-----------|-----|-----|-----|
| -ar | *magna* | *magna* | *magna* |
| -er | *veda* | *veda* | *veda* |
| -ir | *parta* | *parta* | *parta* |
| èsser | *sia* | *sia* | *sia* |
| gavér | *gabia* | *gabia* | *gabia* |

### Imperfect Subjunctive Patterns

| Verb Type | 1sg | 3sg | 3pl |
|-----------|-----|-----|-----|
| -ar | *magnasse* | *magnasse* | *magnasse* |
| -er | *vedésse* | *vedésse* | *vedésse* |
| -ir | *partisse* | *partisse* | *partisse* |
| èsser | *fusse* | *fusse* | *fusse* |
| gavér | *gavésse* | *gavésse* | *gavésse* |

### When Subjunctive Is Required

- After *bisogna che*, *xe meio che*
- After *parché* (purpose), *prima che*, *sensa che*, *bènche*
- In unreal conditional sentences (*se gavésse...*)
- With indefinite antecedents in relative clauses

### When Subjunctive Is Often Replaced

- After *credere*, *pensare*, *sperare* (in casual speech)
- When both clauses have the same subject (use infinitive)
- In everyday conversation (indicative common)
