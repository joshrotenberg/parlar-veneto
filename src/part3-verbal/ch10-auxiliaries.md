# Chapter 10: Auxiliaries - *Èsser* and *Gavér*

The two most important verbs in any language are "to be" and "to have." In Venetian, these are *èsser* (to be) and *gavér* or *aver* (to have). They're used constantly on their own and are essential for forming compound tenses like the *passato prossimo*.

Both verbs are irregular - their forms must be memorized.

## 10.1 *Èsser* (To Be)

### Present Indicative

| Person | Full Form | Short Form | Italian |
|--------|-----------|------------|---------|
| mi | *mi son* | *son* | *sono* |
| ti | *ti te si* / *ti ti xe* | *te si* / *ti xe* | *sei* |
| lu | *lu el xe* | *el xe* | *è* |
| ela | *ela la xe* | *la xe* | *è* |
| nu | *nu semo* | *semo* | *siamo* |
| vu | *vu sè* | *sè* | *siete* |
| lori | *lori i xe* | *i xe* | *sono* |
| lore | *lore le xe* | *le xe* | *sono* |

**Notes:**
- 2sg has two forms: *te si* (more central) and *ti xe* (more Venetian)
- 3sg and 3pl share the form *xe* - the clitic distinguishes them
- *Xe* is pronounced /ze/ (with the voiced sibilant)

### Imperfect

| Person | Form | Italian |
|--------|------|---------|
| mi | *gèro* / *ero* | *ero* |
| ti | *te gèri* / *te eri* | *eri* |
| lu/ela | *el/la gèra* / *el/la era* | *era* |
| nu | *gèrimo* / *erimo* | *eravamo* |
| vu | *gèri* / *eri* | *eravate* |
| lori/lore | *i/le gèra* / *i/le era* | *erano* |

Both *gèra* forms and *era* forms are used, sometimes interchangeably.

## 10.2 *Gavér* / *Aver* (To Have)

Venetian has two forms for "to have": *gavér* (with g-) and *aver* (without). Both are used; *gavér* is perhaps more distinctively Venetian, while *aver* is closer to Italian.

### Present Indicative

| Person | Full Form | Short Form | Italian |
|--------|-----------|------------|---------|
| mi | *mi go* | *go* | *ho* |
| ti | *ti te ga* | *te ga* | *hai* |
| lu | *lu el ga* | *el ga* | *ha* |
| ela | *ela la ga* | *la ga* | *ha* |
| nu | *nu gavemo* / *avemo* | *gavemo* | *abbiamo* |
| vu | *vu gavè* / *avè* | *gavè* | *avete* |
| lori | *lori i ga* | *i ga* | *hanno* |
| lore | *lore le ga* | *le ga* | *hanno* |

**Notes:**
- 1sg, 2sg/3sg/3pl use the short forms *go*, *ga*
- 1pl and 2pl show *gav-* stem in full: *gavemo*, *gavè*
- The g- forms (*go*, *ga*, *gavemo*) are standard; some areas use *o*, *a*, *avemo*

## 10.3 Using the Auxiliaries

### *Èsser* as Main Verb

**Identity:**
- *Son veneto.* (I'm Venetian.)
- *El xe me fradel.* (He's my brother.)
- *Xe ela?* (Is it her?)

**Description:**
- *La casa la xe granda.* (The house is big.)
- *I xe stanchi.* (They're tired.)
- *El vin el xe bon.* (The wine is good.)

**Location:**
- *Dove te si?* (Where are you?)
- *Son a casa.* (I'm at home.)
- *I xe in piassa.* (They're in the square.)

**Time/Date:**
- *Che ora xela?* (What time is it?)
- *Xe le tre.* (It's three o'clock.)
- *Ancò xe luni.* (Today is Monday.)

### *Gavér* as Main Verb

**Possession:**
- *Go na casa.* (I have a house.)
- *Te ga fradéi?* (Do you have brothers?)
- *No gavemo schei.* (We don't have money.)

**Age:**
- *Quanti ani te ga?* (How old are you?)
- *Go trentasìnque ani.* (I'm 35 years old.)

**Physical/emotional states:**
- *Go fame.* (I'm hungry.)
- *Go sé.* (I'm thirsty.)
- *Go fredo.* (I'm cold.)
- *Go caldo.* (I'm hot.)
- *Go sono.* (I'm sleepy.)
- *Te ga rason.* (You're right.)
- *Gavemo fretta.* (We're in a hurry.)

### Existential *ghe xe*

The construction *ghe xe* (there is/are) is extremely common:

| Venetian | Italian | English |
|----------|---------|---------|
| *Ghe xe un problema.* | *C'è un problema.* | There's a problem. |
| *Ghe xe tanti turisti.* | *Ci sono tanti turisti.* | There are many tourists. |
| *No ghe xe gnente.* | *Non c'è niente.* | There's nothing. |
| *Cossa ghe xe?* | *Cosa c'è?* | What's there? / What's wrong? |
| *No ghe xe mal.* | *Non c'è male.* | Not bad. |

**Question form:**
- *Ghe xe-lo?* (Is there?)
- *Ghe xe-li?* (Are there?)

## 10.4 Compound Tenses

The main function of auxiliaries is forming compound tenses, especially the *passato prossimo* (present perfect).

### Choosing the Auxiliary

Like Italian, Venetian uses:
- ***Gavér*** with most transitive verbs (those that take a direct object)
- ***Èsser*** with intransitive verbs of motion/change, reflexive verbs

**With *gavér*:**
| Venetian | Italian | English |
|----------|---------|---------|
| *Go magnà.* | *Ho mangiato.* | I ate / I have eaten. |
| *Te ga parlà.* | *Hai parlato.* | You spoke. |
| *El ga visto.* | *Ha visto.* | He saw. |
| *Gavemo comprà el vin.* | *Abbiamo comprato il vino.* | We bought the wine. |

**With *èsser*:**
| Venetian | Italian | English |
|----------|---------|---------|
| *Son ndà.* / *Son andà.* | *Sono andato.* | I went. |
| *El xe rivà.* | *È arrivato.* | He arrived. |
| *Semo partìi.* | *Siamo partiti.* | We left. |
| *La xe nassùa.* | *È nata.* | She was born. |

**With reflexives (always *èsser*):**
| Venetian | Italian | English |
|----------|---------|---------|
| *Me son lavà.* | *Mi sono lavato.* | I washed (myself). |
| *El se ga vestìo.* | *Si è vestito.* | He got dressed. |
| *Se semo incontrà.* | *Ci siamo incontrati.* | We met. |

### Agreement with *Èsser*

When using *èsser* as auxiliary, the past participle agrees with the subject in gender and number:

| Subject | Past Participle | Example |
|---------|-----------------|---------|
| m. sg. | -à / -ù | *El xe ndà.* |
| f. sg. | -àa / -ùa | *La xe ndàa.* |
| m. pl. | -ài / -ùi | *I xe ndài.* |
| f. pl. | -àe / -ùe | *Le xe ndàe.* |

### Impersonal Construction (Review)

As noted in Chapter 7, when the subject follows the verb (news/announcement), the clitic drops and the participle stays masculine singular:

- *Xe rivà me soreła.* (My sister arrived! - news)
- vs. *Me soreła la xe rivàa.* (My sister has arrived. - topic-comment)

## 10.5 Common Expressions with Auxiliaries

### With *èsser*

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| *Son stufo.* | I'm fed up. |
| *Te si mato!* | You're crazy! |
| *Xe vero.* | It's true. |
| *No xe gnente.* | It's nothing. |
| *Com'ela?* / *Come xeła?* | How's it going? |
| *Xe cussì.* | That's how it is. |
| *Xe ora de andar.* | It's time to go. |

### With *gavér*

| Venetian | English |
|----------|---------|
| *Go voja de...* | I feel like... / I want to... |
| *No go voja.* | I don't feel like it. |
| *Go da far.* | I have things to do. |
| *Te ga rason.* | You're right. |
| *No ga importansa.* | It doesn't matter. |
| *Gavemo finìo.* | We're done. |

## 10.6 Questions and Negation

### Questions with *èsser*

| Statement | Question |
|-----------|----------|
| *Te si stanco.* | *Si-to stanco?* |
| *El xe qua.* | *Xe-lo qua?* |
| *I xe partìi.* | *Xe-li partìi?* |

### Questions with *gavér*

| Statement | Question |
|-----------|----------|
| *Te ga fame.* | *Ga-to fame?* |
| *El ga schei.* | *Ga-lo schei?* |
| *I ga finìo.* | *Ga-li finìo?* |

### Negation

| Affirmative | Negative |
|-------------|----------|
| *Son veneto.* | *No son veneto.* |
| *El xe qua.* | *No el xe qua.* |
| *Go fame.* | *No go fame.* |
| *Te ga rason.* | *No te ga rason.* |

| Use *gavér* | Use *èsser* |
|-------------|-------------|
| Transitive verbs | Motion verbs (*andar*, *vegnir*, *partir*) |

| | Reflexive verbs |
