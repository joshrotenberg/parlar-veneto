# Chapter 12: Past Tenses

Venetian has three main past tenses: the *passato prossimo* (present perfect), the *imperfetto* (imperfect), and the *trapassato prossimo* (pluperfect). Like Italian, Venetian has largely abandoned the *passato remoto* (simple past) in everyday speech, using the *passato prossimo* for all completed past actions regardless of how long ago they occurred.

## 12.1 Passato Prossimo (Present Perfect)

The *passato prossimo* is the workhorse of past narration in Venetian. It's formed with an auxiliary (*gavér* or *èsser*) plus the past participle.

### Formation

| Auxiliary | Past Participle | Example |
|-----------|-----------------|---------|
| *go/ga/gavemo...* | *-à, -ù, -ì* | *Go magnà.* (I ate.) |
| *son/xe/semo...* | *-à, -ù, -ì* | *Son ndà.* (I went.) |

### With *Gavér*

Most transitive verbs (those that take a direct object) use *gavér*:

| Person | Example | English |
|--------|---------|---------|
| mi | *Go magnà.* | I ate. / I have eaten. |
| ti | *Te ga parlà.* | You spoke. |
| lu | *El ga visto.* | He saw. |
| ela | *La ga scrito.* | She wrote. |
| nu | *Gavemo comprà el vin.* | We bought the wine. |
| vu | *Gavè finìo.* | You (pl.) finished. |
| lori | *I ga ciapà el treno.* | They caught the train. |
| lore | *Le ga leto el libro.* | They (f.) read the book. |

**Note:** The clitic (*te*, *el*, *la*, *i*, *le*) is required as always in 2nd and 3rd person.

### With *Èsser*

Intransitive verbs of motion and change of state use *èsser*:

| Person | Example | English |
|--------|---------|---------|
| mi | *Son ndà.* / *Son andà.* | I went. |
| ti | *Te si rivà.* | You arrived. |
| lu | *El xe partìo.* | He left. |
| ela | *La xe nassùa.* | She was born. |
| nu | *Semo vegnùi.* | We came. |
| vu | *Sè restài.* | You (pl.) stayed. |
| lori | *I xe tornài.* | They returned. |
| lore | *Le xe ndàe.* | They (f.) went. |

### Common Verbs with *Èsser*

| Infinitive | Past Participle | Example |
|------------|-----------------|---------|
| *andar/ndar* | *ndà/andà* | *Son ndà a casa.* (I went home.) |
| *vegnir* | *vegnù* | *El xe vegnù tardi.* (He came late.) |
| *partir* | *partìo* | *I xe partìi ieri.* (They left yesterday.) |
| *rivar* | *rivà* | *Semo rivài.* (We arrived.) |
| *restar* | *restà* | *La xe restàa a casa.* (She stayed home.) |
| *tornar* | *tornà* | *Te si tornà?* (Did you come back?) |
| *nàsser* | *nassù* | *Son nassùo a Padova.* (I was born in Padua.) |
| *morir* | *morto* | *El xe morto.* (He died.) |
| *cascar* | *cascà* | *El xe cascà.* (He fell.) |
| *star* | *stà* | *Come te si stà?* (How have you been?) |

### Reflexive Verbs

Reflexive verbs show variation in auxiliary choice (see Chapter 10). Both forms are used:

| With *gavér* | With *èsser* | English |
|--------------|--------------|---------|
| *Me go lavà.* | *Me son lavà.* | I washed myself. |
| *Te te ga vestìo.* | *Te te si vestìo.* | You got dressed. |
| *El se ga svejà.* | *El se xe svejà.* | He woke up. |
| *Se gavemo incontrà.* | *Se semo incontrài.* | We met. |

### Past Participle Agreement

With *èsser*, the past participle agrees with the subject in gender and number:

| Subject | Ending | Example |
|---------|--------|---------|
| m. sg. | *-à / -ù / -ì* | *El xe ndà.* |
| f. sg. | *-àa / -ùa / -ìa* | *La xe ndàa.* |
| m. pl. | *-ài / -ùi / -ìi* | *I xe ndài.* |
| f. pl. | *-àe / -ùe / -ìe* | *Le xe ndàe.* |

With *gavér*, the participle typically stays masculine singular:

- *Go magnà la torta.* (I ate the cake.) - not *magnàa*

However, when a direct object pronoun precedes the verb, agreement is common:

- *La torta? La go magnàa.* (The cake? I ate it.)
- *I libri? Li go letì.* (The books? I read them.)

### Irregular Past Participles

Many common verbs have irregular participles:

| Infinitive | Past Participle | Example |
|------------|-----------------|---------|
| *èsser* | *stà/stài* | *Son stà malà.* (I was sick.) |
| *far* | *fato* | *Cossa te ga fato?* (What did you do?) |
| *dir* | *dito* | *No go dito gnente.* (I didn't say anything.) |
| *véder* | *visto* | *Te ga visto?* (Did you see?) |
| *scriver* | *scrito* | *Go scrito na letera.* (I wrote a letter.) |
| *léxer* | *leto* | *El ga leto el giornal.* (He read the newspaper.) |
| *tòr* | *tolto/ciapà* | *Go tolto i schei.* (I took the money.) |
| *meter* | *messo/metù* | *Dove te lo ga messo?* (Where did you put it?) |
| *vènder* | *vendù* | *I ga vendù la casa.* (They sold the house.) |
| *pèrder* | *perso/perdù* | *Go perso le ciave.* (I lost the keys.) |
| *risponder* | *risposto* | *No el ga risposto.* (He didn't answer.) |
| *romper* | *roto* | *Chi ga roto el goto?* (Who broke the glass?) |
| *còrer* | *corso* | *I ga corso via.* (They ran away.) |
| *pianxer* | *pianto* | *La ga pianto.* (She cried.) |
| *vìnser* | *vinto* | *Gavemo vinto!* (We won!) |

## 12.2 Imperfetto (Imperfect)

The imperfect describes ongoing, habitual, or background actions in the past. Unlike the *passato prossimo*, which presents events as completed, the imperfect presents them as unfolding or repeated.

### Regular Formation

The imperfect is formed by adding endings to the verb stem:

**-ar verbs (*magnar*):**

| Person | Form | English |
|--------|------|---------|
| mi | *magnava* | I was eating / I used to eat |
| ti | *te magnavi* | you were eating |
| lu/ela | *el/la magnava* | he/she was eating |
| nu | *magnàvimo* | we were eating |
| vu | *magnàvi* | you (pl.) were eating |
| lori/lore | *i/le magnava* | they were eating |

**-er verbs (*véder*):**

| Person | Form | English |
|--------|------|---------|
| mi | *vedéa* | I was seeing |
| ti | *te vedévi* | you were seeing |
| lu/ela | *el/la vedéa* | he/she was seeing |
| nu | *vedévimo* | we were seeing |
| vu | *vedévi* | you (pl.) were seeing |
| lori/lore | *i/le vedéa* | they were seeing |

**-ir verbs (*dormir*):**

| Person | Form | English |
|--------|------|---------|
| mi | *dormìa* | I was sleeping |
| ti | *te dormìvi* | you were sleeping |
| lu/ela | *el/la dormìa* | he/she was sleeping |
| nu | *dormìvimo* | we were sleeping |
| vu | *dormìvi* | you (pl.) were sleeping |
| lori/lore | *i/le dormìa* | they were sleeping |

### Imperfect of *Èsser* and *Gavér*

| Person | *èsser* | *gavér* |
|--------|---------|---------|
| mi | *gèro* / *ero* | *gavéa* / *avéa* |
| ti | *te gèri* | *te gavévi* |
| lu/ela | *el/la gèra* | *el/la gavéa* |
| nu | *gèrimo* | *gavévimo* |
| vu | *gèri* | *gavévi* |
| lori/lore | *i/le gèra* | *i/le gavéa* |

Both *gèra* and *era* forms exist for *èsser*; *gèra* is more distinctively Venetian.

### Uses of the Imperfect

**Habitual past actions:**
- *Quando che gèro puteo, xogava sempre in strada.* (When I was a kid, I always played in the street.)
- *Ogni domenega ndàvimo da me nona.* (Every Sunday we went to my grandmother's.)

**Ongoing background actions:**
- *Piové e tirèa vento.* (It was raining and the wind was blowing.)
- *Gèra le sié de sera.* (It was six in the evening.)

**Descriptions in the past:**
- *La casa gèra granda e vècia.* (The house was big and old.)
- *El gavéa i cavei bianchi.* (He had white hair.)

**States and conditions:**
- *Gavéa fame.* (I was hungry.)
- *No stava ben.* (He wasn't well.)
- *No savéa cossa far.* (I didn't know what to do.)

**Interrupted actions:**
- *Magnava quando che xe rivà Marco.* (I was eating when Marco arrived.)
- *Dormìa e me ga ciamà al telefono.* (I was sleeping and they called me.)

### Imperfect vs. Passato Prossimo

The distinction parallels Italian:

| Imperfect | Passato Prossimo |
|-----------|------------------|
| Ongoing, habitual, background | Completed, punctual, foreground |
| *Piové.* (It was raining.) | *Ga piovù.* (It rained.) |
| *Magnava.* (I was eating.) | *Go magnà.* (I ate.) |
| *Studiava sempre.* (I always studied.) | *Go studià ieri.* (I studied yesterday.) |

Often both appear in the same sentence:
- *Magnava quando che el xe rivà.* (I was eating when he arrived.)
- *Gèra le oto quando che son partìo.* (It was eight o'clock when I left.)

## 12.3 Trapassato Prossimo (Pluperfect)

The pluperfect describes actions completed before another past action. It's formed with the imperfect of the auxiliary plus the past participle.

### Formation

| With *gavér* | With *èsser* |
|--------------|--------------|
| *gavéa magnà* | *gèro ndà* |
| *te gavévi parlà* | *te gèri rivà* |
| *el gavéa visto* | *el gèra partìo* |
| *gavévimo finìo* | *gèrimo vegnùi* |
| *gavévi dito* | *gèri restài* |
| *i gavéa fato* | *i gèra tornài* |

### Examples

- *Gavéa xà magnà quando che el xe rivà.* (I had already eaten when he arrived.)
- *No savéa che i gèra xà partìi.* (I didn't know they had already left.)
- *La ga dito che la gavéa visto Marco.* (She said she had seen Marco.)
- *Gèrimo xà ndài via quando che ga scomissià a pióver.* (We had already left when it started raining.)

### Sequence of Tenses

The pluperfect establishes the "earlier past":

| Earlier (Pluperfect) | Later (Passato Prossimo) |
|----------------------|--------------------------|
| *Gavéa studià...* | *...e go passà l'esame.* |
| (I had studied...) | (...and I passed the exam.) |

## 12.4 The Passato Remoto

The *passato remoto* (simple past) exists in Venetian but is rarely used in everyday speech, having been replaced by the *passato prossimo*. You may encounter it in older texts, formal writing, or when discussing distant historical events.

### Sample Forms

**-ar verbs (*magnar*):**
- *magnè* (I ate), *te magnèsti*, *el magnè*, *magnèssimo*, *magnèste*, *i magnè*

**-er verbs (*véder*):**
- *vedèi* (I saw), *te vedèsti*, *el vedè*, *vedèssimo*, *vedèste*, *i vedè*

For practical purposes, learners can focus on the *passato prossimo*, which covers all the same functions in modern spoken Venetian.

## 12.5 Putting It Together

Here's how the past tenses work together in narrative:

> *Ieri gèro a casa, no stava miga ben. Gavéa magnà massa la sera prima e me fasèa mal la pansa. A le diese me ga ciamà Marco e el me ga dito che i ndava al bar. Go dito de sì, me son vestìo e son ndà fora. Quando che son rivà, i gèra xà là, i bevéa el spritz. Go ordinà un'ombra e semo restài fin a mesanote.*

Translation:
> Yesterday I was at home, I wasn't feeling well. I had eaten too much the night before and my stomach hurt. At ten Marco called me and told me they were going to the bar. I said yes, got dressed, and went out. When I arrived, they were already there, drinking spritz. I ordered a glass of wine and we stayed until midnight.

Notice:
- **Imperfect** for states and background: *gèro*, *stava*, *fasèa mal*, *gèra*, *bevéa*
- **Pluperfect** for earlier action: *gavéa magnà*
- **Passato prossimo** for completed events: *ga ciamà*, *ga dito*, *go dito*, *me son vestìo*, *son ndà*, *son rivà*, *go ordinà*, *semo restài*

## Summary

### Quick Reference: Past Tense Formation

| Tense | Formation | Example |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| Passato prossimo | auxiliary (present) + past participle | *Go magnà.* |
| Imperfect | verb stem + imperfect endings | *Magnava.* |
| Pluperfect | auxiliary (imperfect) + past participle | *Gavéa magnà.* |

### Auxiliary Selection

| Use *gavér* | Use *èsser* |
|-------------|-------------|
| Transitive verbs | Motion verbs (*andar*, *vegnir*, *partir*) |
| | Change of state (*nàsser*, *morir*) |
| Reflexives (variation) | Reflexives (variation) |

### Past Participle Patterns

| Verb Class | Regular Ending | Example |
|------------|----------------|---------|
| -ar | -à | *magnà*, *parlà*, *ndà* |
| -er | -ù | *vendù*, *savù*, *avù* |
| -ir | -ì / -ìo | *dormì*, *finìo*, *partìo* |

### Agreement

- With *èsser*: participle agrees with subject (*la xe ndàa*, *i xe ndài*)
- With *gavér*: typically no agreement, but agrees with preceding direct object pronoun (*la go vista*)

### When to Use Each Tense

| Tense | Use For |
|-------|---------|
| Passato prossimo | Completed actions, events, narrative foreground |
| Imperfect | Ongoing states, habits, descriptions, background |
| Pluperfect | Actions completed before another past action |
