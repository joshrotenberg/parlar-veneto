# Chapter 13: Future and Conditional

The future and conditional in Venetian work similarly to Italian, but with some distinctive forms and - importantly - a strong tendency to use the present tense where English and Italian would use the future. Understanding when Venetians actually use these tenses is as important as knowing their forms.

## 13.1 Future Tense

### Formation

The future is formed by adding endings to the infinitive stem (with some modifications). The endings are the same across all verb classes:

| Person | Ending |
|--------|--------|
| mi | *-arò* |
| ti | *-arè* |
| lu/ela | *-arà* |
| nu | *-aremo* |
| vu | *-arè* |
| lori/lore | *-arà* |

**-ar verbs (*magnar*):**

| Person | Form | English |
|--------|------|---------|
| mi | *magnarò* | I will eat |
| ti | *te magnarè* | you will eat |
| lu/ela | *el/la magnarà* | he/she will eat |
| nu | *magnaremo* | we will eat |
| vu | *magnarè* | you (pl.) will eat |
| lori/lore | *i/le magnarà* | they will eat |

**-er verbs (*véder*):**

The *e* of the infinitive typically drops:

| Person | Form | English |
|--------|------|---------|
| mi | *vedarò* | I will see |
| ti | *te vedarè* | you will see |
| lu/ela | *el/la vedarà* | he/she will see |
| nu | *vedaremo* | we will see |
| vu | *vedarè* | you (pl.) will see |
| lori/lore | *i/le vedarà* | they will see |

**-ir verbs (*partir*):**

| Person | Form | English |
|--------|------|---------|
| mi | *partirò* | I will leave |
| ti | *te partirè* | you will leave |
| lu/ela | *el/la partirà* | he/she will leave |
| nu | *partiremo* | we will leave |
| vu | *partirè* | you (pl.) will leave |
| lori/lore | *i/le partirà* | they will leave |

### Irregular Futures

Common verbs have irregular future stems:

| Infinitive | Future Stem | Example (1sg) |
|------------|-------------|---------------|
| *èsser* | *sar-* | *sarò* (I will be) |
| *gavér* | *gavar-* | *gavarò* (I will have) |
| *andar* | *andar-* | *andarò* (I will go) |
| *vegnir* | *vegnar-* | *vegnarò* (I will come) |
| *poder* | *podar-* | *podarò* (I will be able) |
| *voler* | *vorar-* | *vorarò* (I will want) |
| *saver* | *savarò* | *savarò* (I will know) |
| *dover* | *dovar-* | *dovarò* (I will have to) |
| *far* | *far-* | *farò* (I will do) |
| *dir* | *dir-* | *dirò* (I will say) |
| *star* | *star-* | *starò* (I will stay) |
| *dar* | *dar-* | *darò* (I will give) |

### Full Conjugation: *Èsser* and *Gavér*

| Person | *èsser* | *gavér* |
|--------|---------|---------|
| mi | *sarò* | *gavarò* |
| ti | *te sarè* | *te gavarè* |
| lu/ela | *el/la sarà* | *el/la gavarà* |
| nu | *saremo* | *gavaremo* |
| vu | *sarè* | *gavarè* |
| lori/lore | *i/le sarà* | *i/le gavarà* |

### Uses of the Future

**Predictions and expectations:**
- *Doman piovarà.* (Tomorrow it will rain.)
- *El sarà contento.* (He'll be happy.)
- *Gavaremo bel tempo.* (We'll have nice weather.)

**Promises and intentions:**
- *Te ciamerò doman.* (I'll call you tomorrow.)
- *Vegnarò de sicuro.* (I'll definitely come.)
- *No lo farò pi.* (I won't do it again.)

**Conjecture about the present:**
- *Sarà le diese.* (It must be ten o'clock.)
- *El gavarà sinquant'ani.* (He must be about fifty.)
- *Dove sarà-lo?* (Where could he be?)

### The Present Instead of Future

In everyday speech, Venetians often use the present tense for future actions, especially when the context makes the time clear:

| With Present | With Future | English |
|--------------|-------------|---------|
| *Doman vago a Venesia.* | *Doman andarò a Venesia.* | Tomorrow I'm going to Venice. |
| *Stasera magnemo fora.* | *Stasera magnaremo fora.* | Tonight we're eating out. |
| *Te ciamo dopo.* | *Te ciamerò dopo.* | I'll call you later. |
| *Vegno subito.* | *Vegnarò subito.* | I'm coming right away. |

The present is especially common with:
- Time expressions (*doman*, *stasera*, *dopo*)
- Verbs of motion (*andar*, *vegnir*, *partir*)
- Immediate or planned actions

## 13.2 Conditional

The conditional expresses hypothetical situations, polite requests, and desires. It's also used in the consequence clause of conditional sentences.

### Formation

Like the future, the conditional uses the infinitive stem with distinctive endings:

| Person | Ending |
|--------|--------|
| mi | *-arìa* |
| ti | *-arìsi* |
| lu/ela | *-arìa* |
| nu | *-arìsimo* |
| vu | *-arìsi* |
| lori/lore | *-arìa* |

**-ar verbs (*magnar*):**

| Person | Form | English |
|--------|------|---------|
| mi | *magnarìa* | I would eat |
| ti | *te magnarìsi* | you would eat |
| lu/ela | *el/la magnarìa* | he/she would eat |
| nu | *magnarìsimo* | we would eat |
| vu | *magnarìsi* | you (pl.) would eat |
| lori/lore | *i/le magnarìa* | they would eat |

**-er verbs (*véder*):**

| Person | Form | English |
|--------|------|---------|
| mi | *vedarìa* | I would see |
| ti | *te vedarìsi* | you would see |
| lu/ela | *el/la vedarìa* | he/she would see |
| nu | *vedarìsimo* | we would see |
| vu | *vedarìsi* | you (pl.) would see |
| lori/lore | *i/le vedarìa* | they would see |

**-ir verbs (*partir*):**

| Person | Form | English |
|--------|------|---------|

| lu/ela | *el/la partirìa* | he/she would leave |

### Irregular Conditionals

The same verbs with irregular future stems have irregular conditional stems:

| Infinitive | Conditional Stem | Example (1sg) |
|------------|------------------|---------------|

| *vegnir* | *vegnar-* | *vegnarìa* (I would come) |

| *voler* | *vorar-* | *vorarìa* (I would want) |

| *far* | *far-* | *farìa* (I would do) |
| *dir* | *dir-* | *dirìa* (I would say) |

### Full Conjugation: *Èsser* and *Gavér*

| Person | *èsser* | *gavér* |
|--------|---------|---------|
| mi | *sarìa* | *gavarìa* |
| ti | *te sarìsi* | *te gavarìsi* |
| lu/ela | *el/la sarìa* | *el/la gavarìa* |
| nu | *sarìsimo* | *gavarìsimo* |
| vu | *sarìsi* | *gavarìsi* |
| lori/lore | *i/le sarìa* | *i/le gavarìa* |

### Uses of the Conditional

**Polite requests:**
- *Vorarìa un cafè, par piaser.* (I'd like a coffee, please.)
- *Podarìsi dirme che ora xela?* (Could you tell me what time it is?)
- *Gavarìsi da canbiar?* (Would you have change?)

**Wishes and desires:**
- *Me piasarìa ndar in vacansa.* (I'd like to go on vacation.)
- *Vorarìa che el vegnesse.* (I'd like him to come.)
- *Sarìa beo!* (That would be nice!)

**Softened statements:**
- *Dirìa de sì.* (I'd say yes.)
- *Parìa che piova.* (It looks like it might rain.)
- *Gavarìa da èsser qua.* (He should be here.)

**Advice and suggestions:**
- *Dovarìsi parlar con lu.* (You should talk to him.)
- *Podarìsimo provar.* (We could try.)
- *Al posto to, andarìa.* (In your place, I'd go.)

**Reported speech (future in the past):**
- *El ga dito che el sarìa vegnù.* (He said he would come.)
- *Pensava che i gavarìa finìo.* (I thought they would have finished.)

## 13.3 Conditional Sentences

Conditional sentences have an "if" clause (*protasis*) and a result clause (*apodosis*). Venetian follows patterns similar to Italian.

### Real Conditions (Present/Future)

If the condition is possible or likely, use present or future in both clauses:

| Structure | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Se + present, present | *Se piove, stago a casa.* (If it rains, I stay home.) |
| Se + present, future | *Se piove, starò a casa.* (If it rains, I'll stay home.) |
| Se + future, future | *Se piovarà, starò a casa.* (If it rains, I'll stay home.) |

### Unreal Conditions (Present)

For hypothetical present situations, use the imperfect subjunctive in the "if" clause and conditional in the result:

| Structure | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Se + impf. subj., conditional | *Se gavésse schei, comprarìa na casa.* |
| | (If I had money, I'd buy a house.) |

In casual speech, the imperfect indicative often replaces both:

| Formal | Colloquial |
|--------|------------|
| *Se gavésse schei, comprarìa...* | *Se gavéa schei, comprava...* |
| *Se podésse, vegnarìa.* | *Se podéa, vegnéa.* |

This colloquial pattern (imperfect + imperfect) is very common in spoken Venetian.

### Unreal Conditions (Past)

For contrary-to-fact past situations, use the pluperfect subjunctive and past conditional:

| Structure | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Se + plupf. subj., past cond. | *Se gavésse savù, sarìa vegnù.* |
| | (If I had known, I would have come.) |

The past conditional is formed with the conditional of the auxiliary plus past participle:
- *sarìa ndà* (I would have gone)
- *gavarìa fato* (I would have done)
- *se sarìa svejà* (he would have woken up)

## 13.4 Common Expressions

### With Future

| Venetian | English |

## Summary

### Future Formation

| Verb Class | Stem | Endings |
|------------|------|---------|
| -ar | infinitive | *-arò, -arè, -arà, -aremo, -arè, -arà* |
| -er | infin. minus -e | *-arò, -arè, -arà...* |
| -ir | infinitive | *-irò, -irè, -irà, -iremo, -irè, -irà* |

### Conditional Formation

| Verb Class | Stem | Endings |
|------------|------|---------|
| -ar | infinitive | *-arìa, -arìsi, -arìa, -arìsimo, -arìsi, -arìa* |
| -er | infin. minus -e | *-arìa, -arìsi...* |
| -ir | infinitive | *-irìa, -irìsi, -irìa, -irìsimo, -irìsi, -irìa* |

### Key Irregular Stems

| Infinitive | Future/Cond. Stem |
|------------|-------------------|
| *èsser* | *sar-* |
| *gavér* | *gavar-* |
| *poder* | *podar-* |
| *voler* | *vorar-* |
| *vegnir* | *vegnar-* |

### Conditional Sentence Patterns

| Type | If Clause | Result Clause |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| Real | present/future | present/future |
| Unreal (present) | impf. subjunctive (or impf. ind.) | conditional (or impf. ind.) |
| Unreal (past) | pluperfect subjunctive | past conditional |

### When to Use Present Instead of Future

The present tense commonly replaces the future with:
- Time expressions (*doman*, *stasera*, *dopo*)
- Motion verbs (*andar*, *vegnir*)
- Immediate or planned actions
- Informal speech
