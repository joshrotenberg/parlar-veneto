# Chapter 4: Articles

Venetian, like Italian and other Romance languages, has both definite and indefinite articles. These small words agree with their nouns in gender and number. If you know Italian, the system will feel familiar, though the forms differ.

## 4.1 Definite Articles

The definite article ("the") has four forms:

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|----------|--------|
| **Masculine** | el | i |
| **Feminine** | la | le |

### Basic Usage

| Venetian | Italian | English |
|----------|---------|---------|
| *el can* | *il cane* | the dog |
| *la casa* | *la casa* | the house |
| *i cani* | *i cani* | the dogs |
| *le case* | *le case* | the houses |

### Comparison with Italian

The main difference from Italian is the masculine singular: Venetian uses **el** where Italian uses *il*. Italian's multiple masculine forms (*il*, *lo*, *l'*, *gli*) are simplified in Venetian:

| Italian | Venetian | English |
|---------|----------|---------|
| *il libro* | *el libro* | the book |
| *lo stato* | *el stato* | the state |
| *l'uomo* | *l'omo* | the man |
| *gli uomini* | *i omini* | the men |
| *gli stati* | *i stati* | the states |

Venetian does not have a separate form like Italian *lo/gli* before s + consonant, z, gn, etc. It simply uses *el/i* throughout.

### Elision

Before vowels, the singular articles typically elide (lose their vowel):

| Full Form | Elided | Example |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| *el* | *l'* | *l'omo* (the man) |
| *la* | *l'* | *l'aqua* (the water) |

Examples:
- *l'amigo* (the friend, m.)
- *l'ora* (the hour)
- *l'ocio* (the eye)

The plural forms *i* and *le* generally do not elide:
- *i amighi* (the friends, m.)
- *le ore* (the hours)

### Regional Variation

In some areas, you may hear or see:
- **'l** as a reduced form of *el*: *'l can* for *el can*
- **ła** and **łe** with the evanescent L marked: *ła casa*, *łe case*

The pronunciation of *la/le* with the evanescent ł varies by region (see Chapter 1).

## 4.2 Indefinite Articles

The indefinite article ("a, an") has two main forms:

| | Singular |
|---|----------|
| **Masculine** | un |
| **Feminine** | una |

### Basic Usage

| Venetian | Italian | English |
|----------|---------|---------|
| *un can* | *un cane* | a dog |
| *una casa* | *una casa* | a house |
| *un omo* | *un uomo* | a man |
| *una dona* | *una donna* | a woman |

### Elision

The feminine *una* elides before vowels:
- *un'ombra* (a glass of wine, lit. "a shadow")
- *un'ora* (an hour)
- *un'aqua* (a water)

The masculine *un* does not change before vowels:
- *un amigo* (a friend)
- *un oco* (a goose)

### No Plural Indefinite Article

Like Italian, Venetian has no plural indefinite article. Where English uses "some," Venetian either uses no article or the partitive (see below):

- *Go visto cani.* (I saw [some] dogs.)
- *Ghe xe libri sul taolo.* (There are [some] books on the table.)

## 4.3 The Partitive

To express "some" or "any" with a quantity sense, Venetian uses **de** + definite article, contracted:

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|----------|--------|
| **Masculine** | del | dei |
| **Feminine** | de la | de le |

Examples:
- *Vogio del vin.* (I want some wine.)
- *Go comprà dei libri.* (I bought some books.)
- *Ghe xe de la pasta.* (There's some pasta.)
- *Go visto de le tose.* (I saw some girls.)

In casual speech, the partitive is often omitted, especially in the plural:
- *Vogio vin.* (I want wine.)
- *Go comprà libri.* (I bought books.)

## 4.4 Contractions with Prepositions

When definite articles follow common prepositions, they contract:

### With *a* (to, at)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|----------|--------|
| **Masculine** | al | ai |
| **Feminine** | a la | a le |

- *Vado al mercà.* (I'm going to the market.)
- *Digo ai tosi.* (I tell the boys.)

### With *de* (of, from)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|----------|--------|
| **Masculine** | del | dei |
| **Feminine** | de la | de le |

- *El libro del maestro.* (The teacher's book.)
- *La casa dei nonni.* (The grandparents' house.)

### With *da* (from, by, at someone's place)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|----------|--------|
| **Masculine** | dal | dai |
| **Feminine** | da la | da le |

- *Vegno dal dotor.* (I'm coming from the doctor's.)
- *Vado dai amighi.* (I'm going to my friends' place.)

### With *in* (in)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|----------|--------|
| **Masculine** | nel / 'nte'l | nei / 'ntei |
| **Feminine** | ne la / 'nte la | ne le / 'nte le |

- *Nel xardin.* (In the garden.)
- *'Nte la casa.* (In the house.)

### With *su* (on)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|----------|--------|
| **Masculine** | sul | sui |
| **Feminine** | su la | su le |

- *Sul taolo.* (On the table.)
- *Su la carega.* (On the chair.)

### With *con* (with)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|----------|--------|
| **Masculine** | col | coi |
| **Feminine** | con la | con le |

- *Vien col fradel.* (Come with your brother.)
- *Parlo coi tosi.* (I speak with the boys.)

### With *par/per* (for)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|----------|--------|
| **Masculine** | par el / pel | par i / pei |
| **Feminine** | par la | par le |

- *Xe par el toso.* (It's for the boy.)
- *Par la mama.* (For mom.)

## 4.5 Article Usage

### Similar to Italian

Most article usage follows Italian patterns:

**With possessives and family terms:**
- *el me libro* (my book) - article used
- *me mare* (my mother) - article often omitted with close family

**With abstract nouns:**
- *La vita xe bela.* (Life is beautiful.)

**With languages:**
- *El veneto xe na bela lengua.* (Venetian is a beautiful language.)

### With Names

A distinctive feature of Venetian (and northern Italian dialects generally) is using the definite article before women's names:

- *La Maria xe rivà.* (Maria has arrived.)
- *Go visto la Giulia.* (I saw Giulia.)

This is not typically done with men's names:
- *Marco xe rivà.* (Marco has arrived.)
- *Go visto Bepi.* (I saw Bepi/Giuseppe.)

This usage is widespread in spoken Venetian and informal contexts, though it may be avoided in formal writing.

## Summary

| Article Type | Masculine | Feminine |
|--------------|-----------|----------|
| Definite sg. | el | la |
| Definite pl. | i | le |
| Indefinite | un | una |

Key differences from Italian:
- **el** instead of *il/lo/l'*
- **i** instead of *i/gli*
- Simpler system overall with fewer forms
- Article before women's names is common
