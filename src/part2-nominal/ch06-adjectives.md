# Chapter 6: Adjectives

Adjectives in Venetian agree with the nouns they modify in gender and number, just as in Italian. The patterns are largely parallel, with the expected Venetian sound changes applied.

## 6.1 Adjective Agreement

### Four-Form Adjectives (-o/-a/-i/-e)

Most adjectives have four forms:

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|----------|--------|
| **Masculine** | -o | -i |
| **Feminine** | -a | -e |

**Example: *beo/beło* (beautiful)**

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|----------|--------|
| **Masculine** | *beo* / *beło* | *bei* / *beli* |
| **Feminine** | *bea* / *beła* | *bee* / *bełe* |

- *el toso beło* (the beautiful boy)
- *la tosa beła* (the beautiful girl)
- *i tosi beli* (the beautiful boys)
- *le tose bełe* (the beautiful girls)

**More examples:**

| Adjective | m. sg. | f. sg. | m. pl. | f. pl. | English |
|-----------|--------|--------|--------|--------|---------|
| good | *bon* | *bona* | *boni* | *bone* | good |
| bad | *cativo* | *cativa* | *cativi* | *cative* | bad |
| old | *vecio* | *vecia* | *veci* | *vecie* | old |
| new | *novo* | *nova* | *novi* | *nove* | new |
| small | *picolo* | *picola* | *picoli* | *picole* | small |
| big | *grando* | *granda* | *grandi* | *grande* | big |
| red | *rosso* | *rossa* | *rossi* | *rosse* | red |
| white | *bianco* | *bianca* | *bianchi* | *bianche* | white |

### Two-Form Adjectives (-e/-i)

Some adjectives have only two forms (same for masculine and feminine):

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|----------|--------|
| **Both genders** | -e | -i |

**Example: *grande* (big, great)**

- *el omo grande* (the great man)
- *la dona grande* (the great woman)
- *i omini grandi* (the great men)
- *le done grandi* (the great women)

**Common two-form adjectives:**

| Singular | Plural | English |
|----------|--------|---------|
| *grande* | *grandi* | big, great |
| *verde* | *verdi* | green |
| *forte* | *forti* | strong |
| *dolse* | *dolsi* | sweet |
| *triste* | *tristi* | sad |
| *feliçe* | *feliçi* | happy |
| *semplice* | *semplici* | simple |

### Invariable Adjectives

A few adjectives, typically borrowed or truncated forms, don't change:

- *rosa* (pink): *el vestito rosa*, *la camisa rosa*
- *blu* (blue): *el sielo blu*, *la barca blu*
- *par* (equal): always *par*

## 6.2 Position of Adjectives

### Default Position: After the Noun

Unlike English, most Venetian adjectives follow the noun:

- *el libro rosso* (the red book)
- *la casa granda* (the big house)
- *el vin bon* (the good wine)
- *na tosa bea* (a beautiful girl)

### Before the Noun: Common Short Adjectives

A few very common, short adjectives typically precede the noun:

| Adjective | Example | English |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| *beo/beło* | *na beła xornada* | a beautiful day |
| *bon* | *na bona idea* | a good idea |
| *bruto* | *na bruta storia* | an ugly/bad story |
| *caro* | *el me caro amigo* | my dear friend |
| *grando* | *el Gran Canal* | the Grand Canal |
| *longo* | *na longa storia* | a long story |
| *novo* | *na nova casa* | a new house |
| *picolo* | *el picolo toso* | the little boy |
| *povero* | *el povero omo* | the poor man |
| *vecio* | *el vecio ponte* | the old bridge |

### Meaning Changes with Position

Some adjectives change meaning depending on position:

| Before Noun | After Noun |
|-------------|------------|
| *un grando omo* (a great man) | *un omo grando* (a big/tall man) |
| *un vecio amigo* (a longtime friend) | *un amigo vecio* (an elderly friend) |
| *un povero toso* (an unfortunate boy) | *un toso povero* (a poor/broke boy) |
| *un caro amigo* (a dear friend) | *un amigo caro* (an expensive friend?) |

### Multiple Adjectives

When multiple adjectives modify a noun, they typically follow it, joined by *e* (and):

- *na casa granda e bea* (a big and beautiful house)
- *un omo alto e magro* (a tall and thin man)

Or one before and one after:

- *na bea casa granda* (a beautiful big house)

## 6.3 Comparison

### Comparative

**More... than:** *pi ... de/che*

- *Marco xe pi alto de Bepi.* (Marco is taller than Bepi.)
- *Ea xe pi bea de so soreła.* (She is more beautiful than her sister.)
- *Xe pi difisile che beo.* (It's more difficult than beautiful.)

Use *de* before nouns/pronouns, *che* before adjectives, adverbs, infinitives.

**Less... than:** *manco ... de/che*

- *Sto libro xe manco caro de queło.* (This book is less expensive than that one.)

**As... as:** *cussì ... come* or *tanto ... quanto*

- *El xe cussì alto come mi.* (He's as tall as me.)
- *Ea xe tanto brava quanto bea.* (She's as talented as she is beautiful.)

### Superlative

**Relative superlative (the most...):** *el pi ...*

- *Marco xe el pi alto.* (Marco is the tallest.)
- *Ea xe la pi bea de tute.* (She's the most beautiful of all.)
- *I xe i mejo amighi.* (They're the best friends.)

**Absolute superlative (very...):** *-issimo* or *tanto/assai/ben*

- *El xe altissimo.* (He's very tall.)
- *La xe belissima.* (She's very beautiful.)
- *Xe tanto bon!* (It's so good!)
- *Xe assai caro.* (It's very expensive.)
- *Xe ben duro.* (It's really hard.)

### Irregular Comparatives

Some common adjectives have irregular comparative/superlative forms:

| Adjective | Comparative | Superlative | English |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|---------|
| *bon* | *mejo* | *el mejo* / *bonissimo* | good, better, best |
| *cativo* | *pexo* | *el pexo* / *cativissimo* | bad, worse, worst |
| *grando* | *pi grando* / *major* | *el pi grando* / *grandissimo* | big, bigger, biggest |
| *picolo* | *pi picolo* / *minor* | *el pi picolo* / *picolissimo* | small, smaller, smallest |
| *tanto* | *pi* | *el pi* | much, more, most |
| *poco* | *manco* | *el manco* | little, less, least |

Examples:

- *Sto vin xe mejo de queło.* (This wine is better than that one.)
- *Xe el mejo vin de la zona.* (It's the best wine in the area.)
- *Va sempre pexo.* (It keeps getting worse.)

## 6.4 Demonstrative Adjectives

**This/these:** *sto/sta/sti/ste* (or *questo/questa/questi/queste*)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|----------|--------|
| **Masculine** | *sto* | *sti* |
| **Feminine** | *sta* | *ste* |

- *sto libro* (this book)
- *sta casa* (this house)
- *sti tosi* (these boys)
- *ste tose* (these girls)

**That/those:** *queło/queła/quei/quełe*

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|----------|--------|
| **Masculine** | *queło* | *quei* |
| **Feminine** | *queła* | *quełe* |

- *queło libro* (that book)
- *queła casa* (that house)
- *quei tosi* (those boys)
- *quełe tose* (those girls)

The ł in *queło/queła* follows the evanescent L pattern.

## 6.5 Possessive Adjectives

Possessive adjectives agree with the thing possessed, not the possessor:

| Person | Masculine sg. | Feminine sg. | Masculine pl. | Feminine pl. |
|--------|---------------|--------------|---------------|--------------|
| my | *el me* | *la me* | *i me* | *le me* |
| your (sg.) | *el to* | *la to* | *i to* | *le to* |
| his/her/its | *el so* | *la so* | *i so* | *le so* |
| our | *el nostro* | *la nostra* | *i nostri* | *le nostre* |
| your (pl.) | *el vostro* | *la vostra* | *i vostri* | *le vostre* |
| their | *el so* | *la so* | *i so* | *le so* |

Note: *me*, *to*, *so* are invariable for gender and number. The article shows agreement:

- *el me libro* (my book)
- *la me casa* (my house)
- *i me libri* (my books)
- *le me case* (my houses)
- *el to pare* (your father)
- *la so mare* (his/her mother)

### With Family Terms

With close family members, the article is often dropped:

- *me mare* (my mother)
- *to pare* (your father)
- *so fradel* (his/her brother)

But kept with plurals and modified nouns:

- *i me fradei* (my brothers)
- *el me caro nono* (my dear grandfather)

## 6.6 Indefinite Adjectives

Common indefinite adjectives:

| Venetian | Italian | English |
|----------|---------|---------|
| *ogni* | *ogni* | every, each |
| *qualche* | *qualche* | some, a few |
| *tuto/tuta/tuti/tute* | *tutto/a/i/e* | all, every |
| *tanto/tanta/tanti/tante* | *tanto/a/i/e* | much, many |
| *poco/poca/pochi/poche* | *poco/a/hi/he* | little, few |
| *altro/altra/altri/altre* | *altro/a/i/e* | other |
| *certo/certa/certi/certe* | *certo/a/i/e* | certain |
| *qualunque* | *qualunque* | any, whatever |

Examples:

- *Ogni xorno* (every day)
- *Qualche volta* (sometimes)
- *Tuti i tosi* (all the boys)
- *Tanta xente* (many people)
- *Pochi schei* (little money)
- *N'altra volta* (another time)

## Summary

| Feature | Pattern |
|---------|---------|
| Four-form adjectives | -o/-a/-i/-e |
| Two-form adjectives | -e/-i |
| Default position | After noun |
| Common short adjectives | Before noun |
| Comparative | *pi ... de/che* |
| Superlative | *el pi ...* / *-issimo* |
| Demonstratives | *sto/sta/sti/ste*, *queło/queła/quei/quełe* |
| Possessives | *me/to/so* (invariable) + article |
